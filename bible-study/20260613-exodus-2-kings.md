# Exodus & 2 Kings

**Key Verses:** 15:23-25 & 2:19-22

**Main Themes:** Marah: Moses and the Tree

---

## Marah: Moses and the Tree

* The Problem: The Israelites found water at Marah, but they could not drink it because it was too bitter.
* The Action: Moses cried out to God, who showed him a specific piece of wood or a tree.
* The Miracle: Moses threw the wood into the water, and the water became sweet and safe to drink (Exodus 15:23-25). [1, 2, 3, 4, 5] 

## Jericho: Elisha and the Salt

* The Problem: The people of Jericho told Elisha that their city's spring water was bad, causing the land to be unfruitful and miscarriages to occur.
* The Action: Elisha asked for a new bowl filled with salt and threw the salt into the spring.
* The Miracle: The water was permanently healed and purified by God's power (2 Kings 2:19-22). [6, 7, 8, 9, 10] 

## Connection to "Salt of the Earth"
Your mention of "salt of the earth" connects directly to Jesus' teaching in Matthew 5:13. In the ancient world, salt was used to preserve things from corruption and to add flavor. Just as Elisha used salt to heal and purify the toxic water of Jericho, Jesus calls believers to be the "salt of the earth" to bring healing, preservation, and moral purity to a broken world. [11, 12, 13, 14, 15]
